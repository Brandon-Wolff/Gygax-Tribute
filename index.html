The Father of Dungeons and Dragons

Gary Gygax

Gary Gygax (1938-2008) was an American game designer and author best known for co-creating the pioneering role-playing game Dungeons & Dragons. He is often described as the father of D&D.

In 1967, Gary founded the International Federation of Wargamers (IFW) with Bill Speer and Scott Duncan. The next year, he rented a hall in his town of Lake Geneva to hold the first official Lake Geneva Convention, or Gen Con for short. Gen Con is now one of the largest hobby-game gatherings in North America. While the 1968 gathering is technically the first "Official" Gen Con, Gary hosted a 20 person gathering of wargamers in his basement in 1967 that is considered to be "Gen Con 0."

Early in 1970, Gary created Chainmail, a midieval miniature wargame, with Jeff Perren. It included a fantasy supplement, which pioneered many ideas that would later be used in Dungeons & Dragons. Using a modified version of the Chainmail rules, Gygax and Dave Arneson collaborated on what they called "The Fantasy Game," the roleplaying game that would become Dungeons & Dragons.
